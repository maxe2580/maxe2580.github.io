My Teaching Philosophy

Central to my philosophy is the belief that education should inspire students and provide opportunities for every learner to realise their potential. I am committed to social justice in the classroom, ensuring that all students, including those who may struggle in traditional academic settings, have the chance to succeed and feel proud of their achievements.

In my woodwork teaching, I integrate hands-on, project-based learning that fosters creativity, problem-solving, and practical skill development. I intentionally create inclusive, accessible, and supportive learning environments, while embedding awareness of sustainability, craftsmanship, and ethical practice.

I aim to model lifelong learning by exploring new materials, techniques, and industry practices, and by continuously reflecting on and refining my own teaching. My goal is for students to leave my classroom not only with skills and knowledge, but with confidence, curiosity, and a sense of what they are capable of achieving.

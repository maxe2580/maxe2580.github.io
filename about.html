About Me

My name is Max Einspinner. I live in Mona Vale on Sydney’s Northern Beaches and enjoy spending time outdoors, especially surfing, camping, and exploring new places. I am currently studying at the Australian Catholic University, undertaking a double degree in Secondary Education and Design and Technology. In my free time, I love designing and creating furniture and products, combining creativity with practical skills.

My Passion for Teaching

My passion for teaching comes from my own experiences in school. I was not a high-achieving student, and many of my friends left school early. What changed things for me was having teachers who genuinely believed in me and encouraged me to believe in myself. Their support showed me that with the right guidance and confidence, students can achieve far more than they think both in school and in life. I want to be that kind of teacher for my students, helping them recognise their potential and giving them the self-belief to succeed.

My Teaching Experience

I graduated from school in 2022 and was awarded a scholarship from the Catholic Schools of Broken Bay, which led to my first role in education at Mater Maria Catholic College. For two years, I worked in learning support, gaining valuable experience in helping students with diverse needs. I then transferred to St Paul’s Manly, where I taught Stage 5 and Stage 6 Design and Technology and Timber. During my time there, I successfully guided a major work class of Timber students, an experience that strengthened my passion for teaching. Alongside my school roles, I have been studying at university since 2023 and am on track to graduate in 2026. Outside of the classroom, I have worked at Carlile Swimming since 2021, teaching squads, supervising shifts, and fostering a positive and disciplined environment for young swimmers.

My Area of Expertise

My area of expertise is timber technology, which is my major at university and something I have been passionate about since completing my own major work in 2022. Since then, I have developed a strong interest in high-end furniture making, creating small projects in my spare time. Working within the constraints of limited time and resources has pushed me further into the Design and Technology space, where I enjoy solving real-world problems with specific users in mind. In addition to timber, I have experience in food technology, which is my university minor. I have both participated in and taught food classes, giving me a broad skill set across multiple practical subject areas.
